## Why Focus on Computer Graphics?

Computer graphics begins with a moment of wonder: an image that catches your eye and makes you ask, "How is this possible?" From that first spark, you are led into a world of fascinating mathematics, where geometry, light, motion, and patterns reveal deep ideas in a concrete and beautiful way.

Even if it does not seem interesting at first, ignoring computer graphics means missing countless opportunities to better understand the world around us, and to explore one of the most powerful abstractions ever created by Homo sapiens: mathematics.

How many people regret never learning to play the piano? In the same way, it is a luxury to live in an era where we have enough time to be amazed and to understand. It is an infinite opportunity, and we should know how to make the most of it.

In his book Sapiens, Yuval Noah Harari argues that our ability to believe in shared fictions is what makes Homo sapiens exceptional. I agree, but for me science and mathematics hold a special place in that world of social fictions such as religions, economies, and legal entities. They help us remain rational despite our natural tendency to treat non-existent things as if they were real.
